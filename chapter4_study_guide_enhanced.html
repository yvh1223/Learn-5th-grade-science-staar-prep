🧊 States of Matter

"Soldiers Get Loose"
🧊 Solid = particles close together, vibrate in place
💧 Gas = particles far apart, move freely
🌊 Liquid = particles close, slide past each other
Temperature determines state: Hot = gas, Cool = liquid, Cold = solid

Solid Properties:

  • Definite shape and volume
  • Particles tightly packed
  • Vibrate but don't move around
  • Example: Ice, rocks, wood

Liquid Properties:

  • Definite volume, no definite shape
  • Takes shape of container
  • Particles slide past each other
  • Example: Water, milk, oil

Gas Properties:

  • No definite shape or volume
  • Fills entire container
  • Particles spread out and move fast
  • Example: Air, helium, steam

🔄 Physical vs Chemical Changes

"Physical = Reversible, Chemical = Permanent"
🔄 Physical: Can change back (ice ↔ water)
⚗️ Chemical: New substance formed (can't undo)
Look for clues: Color change, gas/smoke, heat/light = CHEMICAL
State change, size change, shape change = PHYSICAL

Physical Changes:

  • Melting: Solid → Liquid
  • Freezing: Liquid → Solid
  • Evaporating: Liquid → Gas
  • Condensing: Gas → Liquid
  • Breaking, cutting, mixing

Chemical Changes:

  • Burning: Paper → ash + smoke
  • Rusting: Iron + oxygen → rust
  • Cooking: Raw egg → cooked egg
  • Digesting: Food → nutrients

⚡ Forms of Energy

"My Little Horse Can Sound Electrical"
🏃 Mechanical (motion)
💡 Light (from sun, bulbs)
🔥 Heat (thermal)
🧪 Chemical (in food, batteries)
🔊 Sound (vibrations)
⚡ Electrical (flowing electrons)
Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed from one form to another

Energy Examples:

  • Mechanical: Moving car, spinning fan
  • Light: Sunlight, flashlight, fire
  • Heat: Hot stove, warm hands, sun
  • Chemical: Food, gasoline, batteries
  • Sound: Music, talking, clapping
  • Electrical: Lightning, outlets, circuits

🔋 Energy Transformations

Common STAAR question: "What energy transformation occurs in a flashlight?"
Answer: Chemical (battery) → Electrical → Light
Follow the energy path: Where does it start? Where does it end?

Common Transformations:

  • Flashlight: Chemical → Electrical → Light
  • Car: Chemical → Mechanical + Heat
  • Solar panel: Light → Electrical
  • Microphone: Sound → Electrical
  • Speaker: Electrical → Sound
  • Eating food: Chemical → Mechanical + Heat

Energy Transfer Methods:

  • Conduction: Heat through touch (spoon in hot soup)
  • Convection: Heat through moving liquids/gases
  • Radiation: Heat through space (sun's warmth)

📝 Quick Practice Questions

Q1: When ice melts, is this a physical or chemical change?
Answer: Physical change (can be reversed by freezing)
Q2: What energy transformation occurs in a flashlight?
Answer: Chemical → Electrical → Light
Q3: Which state of matter has no definite shape or volume?
Answer: Gas
Q4: Burning paper is an example of what type of change?
Answer: Chemical change (new substances formed)

🎯 STAAR Test Strategies for Matter & Energy

State Changes: All involve temperature - hot = gas, cold = solid
Chemical vs Physical: Can you reverse it easily? If yes = physical
Energy Transformations: Follow the path from input to output
Properties: Solid = definite shape, Liquid = takes container shape, Gas = fills space
