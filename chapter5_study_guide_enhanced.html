💧 The Water Cycle

"Every Cloud Produces Rain"
☀️ Evaporation (water → vapor)
☁️ Condensation (vapor → droplets)
🌧️ Precipitation (rain, snow, hail)
🏞️ Runoff/Collection (back to water bodies)
The SUN is the main energy source for the water cycle!

Water Cycle Steps:

  • Evaporation: Sun heats water → becomes water vapor
  • Condensation: Water vapor cools → forms clouds
  • Precipitation: Water falls as rain, snow, sleet, hail
  • Collection: Water flows back to oceans, lakes, rivers

🌤️ Weather vs Climate

"Weather is NOW, Climate is NEVER-ending"
🌤️ Weather = what's happening today
🌍 Climate = average weather over many years
Weather = short-term, Climate = long-term average

Weather (Short-term):

  • Daily conditions
  • Temperature, precipitation, wind
  • Example: "It's raining today"

Climate (Long-term):

  • Average conditions over 30+ years
  • Patterns for a region
  • Example: "Texas is hot and humid"

☁️ Cloud Types

"Clouds Stack in Curly Nimbles"
☁️ Cumulus = puffy, fair weather
🌫️ Stratus = layered, gray
🌪️ Cirrus = wispy, high
⛈️ Nimbus = rain clouds
"Nimbus" in cloud name = RAIN CLOUD

Cloud Types:

  • Cumulus: Puffy, cotton-like, fair weather
  • Stratus: Flat layers, gray, light rain
  • Cirrus: Thin, wispy, high altitude
  • Cumulonimbus: Tall, dark, thunderstorms

📝 Quick Practice Questions

Q1: What is the main source of energy for the water cycle?
Answer: The Sun
Q2: What's the difference between weather and climate?
Answer: Weather = daily conditions, Climate = long-term average
Q3: Which process changes water vapor back to liquid?
Answer: Condensation
Q4: What type of clouds bring thunderstorms?
Answer: Cumulonimbus
