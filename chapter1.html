üîç The Scientific Method - Your Detective Steps!

Follow these 6 amazing steps to think like a real scientist!

1

ü§î ASK A QUESTION

What do you want to know? Start with something you're curious about!

Example: "Why do some plants grow faster than others?"
2

üìö DO RESEARCH

What do other people already know? Learn from experts and books!

Example: Read books, ask experts, search online about plant growth
3

üí≠ MAKE A HYPOTHESIS

Your best educated guess about what will happen! Use "If...then...because" format.

Example: "If plants get more sunlight, then they will grow faster because sunlight helps them make food"
4

üß™ TEST WITH EXPERIMENT

Design a fair test to check your hypothesis. Only change ONE thing!

Example: Grow identical plants - some with lots of light, some with little light
5

üìä ANALYZE RESULTS

What happened? Look at your data carefully and find patterns!

Example: Measure and compare plant heights after 2 weeks. Make graphs and charts!
6

üéØ DRAW CONCLUSIONS

What did you learn? Was your hypothesis correct? Share your discoveries!

Example: "My hypothesis was correct! Plants with more sunlight grew 3 inches taller."

üî¨ Observations vs. Inferences - Detective Skills!

üëÅÔ∏è OBSERVATIONS

What they are: Things you can see, hear, smell, touch, or taste

Examples:

  • The plant is 15 cm tall
  • The liquid is blue
  • The rock feels rough
  • The bird has red feathers
  • The water is 25¬∞C

Remember: Observations are FACTS you can measure or sense!

üß† INFERENCES

What they are: Conclusions based on observations

Examples:

  • The plant grew because it had sunlight
  • The liquid is blue because of food coloring
  • The rock is rough because of weathering
  • The bird is male because of bright colors
  • The water feels warm because it was heated

Remember: Inferences are EXPLANATIONS based on what you observe!

üìè Quantitative vs. Qualitative Data

üî¢ QUANTITATIVE DATA

Uses NUMBERS and measurements

Examples:

  • The plant is 23 cm tall
  • Temperature is 25¬∞C
  • Mass is 150 grams
  • Volume is 50 mL
  • There are 12 birds

Tools: Ruler, balance, thermometer, graduated cylinder

üëÅÔ∏è QUALITATIVE DATA

Uses DESCRIPTIONS and qualities

Examples:

  • The plant is tall and green
  • The water feels warm
  • The rock is heavy and smooth
  • The liquid smells sweet
  • The birds are singing loudly

Tools: Your five senses (sight, smell, touch, taste, hearing)!

üß™ Variables in Experiments

When doing experiments, scientists control different parts called variables:

üéõÔ∏è Independent Variable

What YOU change on purpose

Example: Amount of sunlight given to plants

Remember: The cause in cause-and-effect

üìä Dependent Variable

What YOU measure or observe

Example: Height of the plants after 2 weeks

Remember: The effect in cause-and-effect

üîí Constants

What STAYS exactly the same

Example: Same water, soil, temperature, pot size

Remember: Keep everything else equal!

üéØ Control Group

The "normal" comparison group

Example: Plants with regular sunlight amount

Remember: What you compare your results to

üß™ Try This Experiment: Paper Airplane Science!

ü§î Question: Which paper airplane design flies the farthest?

üìö Research: Look up different airplane designs online or in books. Learn about aerodynamics!

üí≠ Hypothesis: Write your prediction using "If...then...because" format:
"If I use the _____ design, then it will fly farthest because _____"

üß™ Materials: Different types of paper, ruler, tape, open space (like a hallway or gym)

üìã Procedure:

  1. Make 3 different airplane designs using the same paper
  2. Find a long, open space for testing
  3. Mark a starting line with tape
  4. Fly each airplane 5 times from the same spot
  5. Measure how far each flight goes with a ruler
  6. Record your data in a table
  7. Calculate the average distance for each design

üìä Analyze: Which design flew farthest on average? Make a bar graph of your results!

üéØ Conclude: Was your hypothesis correct? What did you learn about airplane design?

üî¨ Variables in this experiment:

  • Independent Variable: Type of airplane design (what you change)
  • Dependent Variable: Distance the airplane flies (what you measure)
  • Constants: Same paper type, same throwing force, same location, same person throwing
  • Control Group: A simple, basic paper airplane design
