üîç The Scientific Method - Your Detective Steps!

1

ü§î ASK A QUESTION

What do you want to know? Start with something you're curious about!

Example: "Why do some plants grow faster than others?"
2

üìö DO RESEARCH

What do other people already know? Learn from experts!

Example: Read books, ask experts, search online about plant growth
3

üí≠ MAKE A HYPOTHESIS

Your best educated guess about what will happen!

Example: "I think plants with more sunlight will grow faster because sunlight helps them make food"
4

üß™ TEST WITH EXPERIMENT

Design a fair test to check your hypothesis

Example: Grow identical plants - some with lots of light, some with little light
5

üìä ANALYZE RESULTS

What happened? Look at your data carefully!

Example: Measure and compare plant heights after 2 weeks
6

üéØ DRAW CONCLUSIONS

What did you learn? Was your hypothesis correct?

Example: "My hypothesis was correct! Plants with more sunlight grew 3 inches taller."

üî¨ Observations vs. Inferences - Detective Skills!

üëÅÔ∏è OBSERVATIONS

What they are: Things you can see, hear, smell, touch, or taste

Examples:

  • The plant is 15 cm tall
  • The liquid is blue
  • The rock feels rough
  • The bird has red feathers

Remember: Observations are FACTS!

üß† INFERENCES

What they are: Conclusions based on observations

Examples:

  • The plant grew because it had sunlight
  • The liquid is blue because of food coloring
  • The rock is rough because of weathering
  • The bird is male because of bright colors

Remember: Inferences are EXPLANATIONS!

üî¢ QUANTITATIVE

  • The plant is 23 cm tall
  • Temperature is 25¬∞C
  • Mass is 150 grams
  • Volume is 50 mL

üëÅÔ∏è QUALITATIVE

  • The plant is tall and green

  • The liquid smells sweet

üß™ Try This Experiment: Paper Airplane Science!

ü§î Question: Which paper airplane design flies the farthest?

üìö Research: Look up different airplane designs online or in books

üí≠ Hypothesis: Write your prediction: "I think the _____ design will fly farthest because _____"

üß™ Materials: Different types of paper, ruler, tape, open space

üìã Procedure:

  1. Make 3 different airplane designs
  2. Fly each airplane 5 times from the same spot
  3. Measure how far each flight goes
  4. Record your data in a table
  5. Calculate the average distance for each design

üìä Analyze: Which design flew farthest on average?

üéØ Conclude: Was your hypothesis correct? What did you learn?

üî¨ Variables:

  • Independent: Type of airplane design
  • Dependent: Distance the airplane flies
  • Constants: Same paper type, same throwing force, same location
