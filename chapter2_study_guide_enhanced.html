🌍 Earth's Layers

"Can My Orange Ice cream"
🍰 Crust (thinnest, where we live)
🍊 Mantle (thickest, hot rock)
🍊 Outer core (liquid iron)
🍨 Inner core (solid iron, hottest)
Most STAAR questions ask: "Which layer is thickest?" Answer: MANTLE

Key Facts:

  • Crust: 5-40 km thick, solid rock
  • Mantle: 2,900 km thick, hot melted rock
  • Outer Core: Liquid iron and nickel
  • Inner Core: Solid iron, 5,000°C

☀️ Solar System

"My Very Educated Mother Just Served Us Nachos"
Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune
Common question: "Which planet is closest to the Sun?" Answer: MERCURY

Planet Facts:

  • Mercury: Closest to Sun, no atmosphere
  • Venus: Hottest planet, thick clouds
  • Earth: Only planet with life
  • Mars: Red planet, has ice caps
  • Jupiter: Largest planet, gas giant
  • Saturn: Has rings, gas giant
  • Uranus: Tilted on its side
  • Neptune: Farthest from Sun, windy

🌓 Day and Night

Key question: "What causes day and night?" Answer: Earth's ROTATION on its axis (24 hours)
Rotation vs Revolution:
🔄 Rotation = spinning like a top (day/night)
🔁 Revolution = going around the Sun (seasons)

Important Facts:

  • Earth spins once every 24 hours
  • Side facing Sun = day
  • Side away from Sun = night
  • Sun appears to move across sky (but Earth is spinning)

🌙 Moon Phases

"New moon Waxes to Full, then Wanes back to New"
New → Waxing Crescent → First Quarter → Waxing Gibbous → Full →
Waning Gibbous → Last Quarter → Waning Crescent → New
Remember: Waxing = getting bigger, Waning = getting smaller

Moon Facts:

  • Moon doesn't make its own light
  • We see reflected sunlight
  • Takes 29.5 days for complete cycle
  • Same side always faces Earth

📝 Quick Practice Questions

Q1: Which layer of Earth is the thickest?
Answer: Mantle
Q2: What causes day and night on Earth?
Answer: Earth's rotation on its axis
Q3: Which planet is closest to the Sun?
Answer: Mercury
Q4: How long does it take Earth to rotate once?
Answer: 24 hours (1 day)
