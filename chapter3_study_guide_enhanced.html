üî¨ Plant vs Animal Cells

"Plants Can Wall, Animals Cannot"
üå± Plant cells have: Cell Wall, Chloroplasts
üêæ Animal cells have: Centrioles (but no wall/chloroplasts)
Common question: "What do plant cells have that animal cells don't?" Answer: CELL WALL and CHLOROPLASTS

Both Have:

  • Cell membrane: Controls what goes in/out
  • Nucleus: Controls the cell (brain)
  • Cytoplasm: Jelly-like substance
  • Mitochondria: Makes energy

Only Plants Have:

  • Cell wall: Rigid outer layer
  • Chloroplasts: Make food from sunlight
  • Large vacuole: Stores water

üå± Photosynthesis

"Plants Eat Light for Lunch"
Sunlight + Water + Carbon Dioxide ‚Üí Sugar + Oxygen
Key equation: 6CO‚ÇÇ + 6H‚ÇÇO + sunlight ‚Üí C‚ÇÜH‚ÇÅ‚ÇÇO‚ÇÜ + 6O‚ÇÇ
(Don't memorize formula, understand the process!)

What Plants Need:

  • Sunlight: Energy source
  • Water: From roots
  • Carbon dioxide: From air through leaves
  • Chlorophyll: Green chemical that captures light

What Plants Make:

  • Sugar (glucose): Food for the plant
  • Oxygen: Released into air (we breathe it!)

üçÉ Food Chains & Webs

"Some People Can Dance"
üå± Sun ‚Üí Producers ‚Üí Consumers ‚Üí Decomposers
Arrows in food chains show ENERGY FLOW, not "who eats who"
Grass ‚Üí Rabbit means energy flows FROM grass TO rabbit

Energy Levels:

  • Producers: Plants (make their own food)
  • Primary consumers: Plant eaters (herbivores)
  • Secondary consumers: Meat eaters (carnivores)
  • Decomposers: Break down dead things

Example Food Chain:

Sun ‚Üí Grass ‚Üí Rabbit ‚Üí Fox ‚Üí Bacteria

ü¶é Adaptations

Adaptation = body part or behavior that helps survive
Physical (body parts) vs Behavioral (actions)
"Animals Adapt to Survive"
Think: How does this help the animal live?

Physical Adaptations:

  • Thick fur: Keeps warm in cold places
  • Webbed feet: Helps swim
  • Sharp claws: Helps catch prey
  • Camouflage: Helps hide

Behavioral Adaptations:

  • Migration: Moving to find food/warmth
  • Hibernation: Sleeping through winter
  • Pack hunting: Working together
  • Building nests: Protecting babies
